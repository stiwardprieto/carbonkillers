In the graphics we can see a map of the data taken from the OCO (Orbiting Carbon Observatory), which is a mission by NASA designed to collect space-based measurements of atmospheric carbon dioxide. By looking at the colorbar, which represents the values of CO2 in ppm, we can see an increasing behaviour on the CO2 through the years, but looking at these colours might not be so clear, so lets show numbers.

This is a numerical representation of the maps shown before. Here we clearly see the alarming rising of the amount of CO2 in the atmosphere through the years, and this shows that something is happening. Now going back to the colormap, we can see that the main sources come from highly industrialized countries, and comparing Colombia to the scale, it’s a little under the global average, which means that compared to other countries, we do not represent a big contribution to the global carbon footprint.

In actuality, Colombia has been working in different strategies and international and national agreements that try to mitigate the impact generated by the GHG, adapt the communities to these changes and reduce the risk that the climate imbalance in a big scale brings. The most importants agreements and commitments in the country against this problematic are:
  • Determined contributions at a national level (DCN), those are the commitments that countries have contributed to reduce the greenhouse effect emissions.
  • National Development Plan (NDP)
  • National climate change policy (NCCP)
  • Sustainable Development Goals
There are some integral plans to manage the climate change called the PIGCC, those search solutions based in the sustainable development in each region of the country. In the Cauca Department there are four main strategies:
  • Cauca, hidraulic pantry
  • Resilient environments
  • Biodiversity and ecosystems
  • Cauca, productive and sustainable
